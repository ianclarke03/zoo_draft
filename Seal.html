Seal

Seals, charismatic members of the pinniped family, are known for their playful behavior and streamlined bodies adapted to aquatic life. With flipper-like limbs, seals are skilled swimmers, from the massive elephant seals to the agile harbor seals. Their soulful eyes and distinctive whiskers add to their charm. Spending most of their lives in the water, seals come ashore for breeding. Despite their gentle appearance, seals are formidable marine predators, playing a crucial role in ocean ecosystems by feeding on fish, squid, and crustaceans.
